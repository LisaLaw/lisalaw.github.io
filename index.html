Hello! My name is Lisa and I am a web developer.
I started my programming journey at the beginning of 2020.

I had already made the choice to specialize in Computational Linguistics during my B.A. in 2017, when I had the amazing opportunity to spend a year at UCLA.

For a while, I worked as a Project Manager in translations. This taught me multitasking skills and what it's like to work in a fast-paced environment.
Next, I completed a Master's level program in education, a Post Graduate Certificate of Education (PGCE) to teach German, Spanish and French as foreign languages. Before I knew it, I was working with teenagers. It was an invaluable experience which taught me a lot about me patience and looking at a problem from different angles.
During this time, I felt an increased need to create, to develop and to work with challenging, more analytical tasks.
I felt a calling and I decided to teach myself the art of developing.

I have some experience teaching myself as I have a love for languages and have previously learned Spanish and French mostly on my own. I love working on a new language - be it spoken or computational -, working out the intrinsic details and underlying logical structures.

So at the beginning of 2020 I started my Python journey with online courses. I have been hooked every since.
Since, I have been working with Python, Django, and JavaScript.

Jump straight to my experiences.

If you want to learn more about me and my thoughts, don't forget to check out my blog!
For more technical writings, please have a look at my medium profile, where I've been publishing for my current company.
